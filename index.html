Section One Climate Trigger
Rainfall Pressure

Rainfall Builds Before the Losses Appear

Rainfall does not build evenly across the year. The five-year timeline first shows when monthly rainfall becomes heaviest, while the seasonal profile reveals which months usually carry the strongest rainfall load.

This section then links rainfall totals with heavy-rain days, showing that flood risk grows not only from how much rain falls, but also from how often rain arrives in intense bursts.

Data Source
World Bank Group · Climate Change Knowledge Portal
climateknowledgeportal.worldbank.org
1.1 When Rainfall Pressure Peaks
The heaviest rain arrives late

Rainfall rises most sharply in late 2024, showing how the Northeast Monsoon can turn the end of the year into the main pressure point for flood risk.

1.2 The Seasonal Shape of Rainfall
November marks the monsoon build-up

November records the highest average rainfall because it sits at the start of the Northeast Monsoon, when seasonal winds begin bringing sustained moisture toward Malaysia.

The peak continues into December, showing that flood risk does not rise from one isolated wet month, but from a late-year build-up of repeated heavy rainfall.

1.3 Rainfall Burst Pattern
Repeated Bursts Raise Flood Pressure

The upward trend shows that months with higher rainfall totals usually also record more heavy-rain days. Flood pressure builds when rain arrives repeatedly in intense bursts, not only from one large monthly total.

Monsoon months appear more often on the higher-rainfall side of the chart, linking the seasonal rainfall surge to stronger short-term flood intensity.

Section Two The Anatomy of Flood Damage
Damage Structure

Behind Every Flooded State, the Damage Pattern Is Different

This section breaks flood damage into its internal structure. It shows which sectors drive losses, where damage is concentrated within key districts, and how severe losses become when measured against exposed living quarters.

Data Source

2.1 State x Sector Loss Matrix
Reading the Damage Pattern
Darker red cells show larger flood losses. Compare each row to see which sector affects a state most and compare each column to see where a sector causes the greatest damage.
manufacturing loss highly concentrated

Kelantan carried 93.8% of manufacturing flood loss in 2024, while Terengganu carried 100% in 2025.

As Kelantan and Terengganu sit along Malaysia's east coast flood belt where Northeast Monsoon flooding often causes severe river overflow.
This makes manufacturing sites, transport routes and local supply chains more exposed when floodwaters spread beyond residential areas.

2.2 Top-District Damage
Reading the District Cluster

Larger symbols and positions further right both show stronger district concentration. Hover to see the actual flood loss value in RM '000.

Flood damage can cluster locally

The repeated house symbol shows that the top-loss districts are mainly dominated by Living Quarters damage. A district can record a large loss value but still have a lower share if the state's flood damage is spread across many districts.

A smaller loss can show a higher percentage when most of the state's flood damage is concentrated in one district.

Section Three The Geography of Loss
State-Level Burden

Some States Carry More of the National Flood Burden

Flood damage concentrates in specific parts of Malaysia rather than spreading evenly across the country. In 2024, the top three states account for more than two-thirds of national flood loss. Selecting a state reveals the category mix behind its total loss, linking state ranking with damage composition.

Data Source
Ministry of Economy Department of Statistics Malaysia
www.dosm.gov.my
3.1 State Share of National Flood Loss (%)
Loss is concentrated in a few states

In 2024, flood loss is strongly concentrated in Kelantan, Kedah, and Terengganu. Together, these three states account for about 68.4% of the national recorded flood loss.

The map shows that national flood impact is not evenly spread. The darkest states form the main loss belt, meaning a small number of states carry most of the flood damage burden.

3.2 State Ranking & Loss Composition
High totals hide different damage mixes

In 2024, Kelantan records the highest total loss, but its damage is not from one category only. Living quarters, infrastructure, agriculture, and business premises all contribute to the total.

The linked donut helps explain why state rankings alone are not enough. For example, Kedah ranks high mainly because of agricultural loss, while Terengganu is driven more by living quarters and infrastructure.

Section Four Agricultural Flood Losses
Agricultural Deep Dive

The Harvest Lost to Floodwaters

Agricultural flood loss is first compared across all states, then narrowed into a selected-state view. The stacked chart shows how each state's agricultural damage is divided by category, while the detail chart reveals the actual loss value and whether each category rose or fell from 2024 to 2025.

Data Source
Ministry of Economy Department of Statistics Malaysia
www.dosm.gov.my
4.1 Agricultural Loss Composition Across States
Paddy loss dominates the agricultural picture

In 2024, agricultural flood loss is dominated by Kedah, where paddy loss makes up most of the state’s agricultural damage. This makes the 2024 agricultural pattern highly concentrated.

In 2025, the pattern becomes more distributed across states such as Johor, Selangor, Kedah, and Kelantan. Fisheries and crops become more visible, while paddy loss no longer dominates the chart.
